# Use cases

Organizations use BCDA claims data to improve care outcomes, reduce costs, and close gaps in their understanding of enrollee health histories. Here are the most common use cases.

## Reduce readmissions with faster discharge visibility

With partially adjudicated claims, your organization can identify hospital discharges within days instead of weeks. This enables timely post-discharge follow-up, care transition planning, and readmission risk monitoring.

Previously, organizations wouldn't know about a discharge until fully adjudicated claims arrived — often 14 or more days later. By then, the critical window for intervention had passed. With BCDA's 2-4 day turnaround, care coordinators can set up automated alerts for newly identified discharges and follow up while it still makes a difference.

## Identify opportunities for clinical intervention

Track outpatient procedures and services that may signal upcoming high-cost events. By monitoring claims patterns, your organization can flag procedures that merit review and activate protocols for primary care intervention before unnecessary or harmful procedures occur.

This is especially valuable for identifying cases where planned procedures may not align with clinical guidelines — allowing your team to intervene early, reducing both the volume of unnecessary procedures and associated costs.

## Fill gaps in enrollee health histories

Medicare enrollees frequently receive care outside your network. Without BCDA, your organization may have no visibility into these encounters. BCDA claims data supplements EMR, ADT feeds, and other sources to provide a more complete picture of each enrollee's health history.

This is particularly valuable for tracking emergency visits, out-of-network specialist care, and prescription drug activity. When an enrollee receives care at a facility 50 miles from home, BCDA ensures your organization learns about it in days — not weeks — enabling timely adjustments to care plans.

## Improve financial forecasting and performance tracking

Access to weekly claims data enables more accurate and timely financial analysis. Your organization can track utilization, shared cost savings, and performance metrics with data that's days old rather than months old.

Partially adjudicated claims data, which updates daily, is particularly useful for monitoring high-cost events in near real-time and adjusting care management strategies accordingly.
